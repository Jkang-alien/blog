Compact is a property of a space. In a nutshell, if a space is compact, we can treat the space be a finite because the space has a finite subcover. A continuous function on a compact space is uniformly continous.

Heine-Borel theorem describes the condition of compactness of finite dimentional space. Closed and bounded But the Heine-Borel theorem does not hold in a infinite dimentional space. We need another conditions.

Previously, the compact space can be finite by taking subcover. The infinite dimentional space can be finite by projection to finite dimention. If we could make as small as possible (i.e. \(\epsilon > 0\) ) the norm of \((X\backslash(1-P)\), the compactness is achieved.

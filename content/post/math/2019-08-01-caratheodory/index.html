The studing sometimes starts with learning of boring preceding concepts. The highlight comes later. In the history, the highlight concepts or the important problem was centered and the supporting concepts or lemmas followed. The one of central ideas of analysis is extension. The set of rational number (\(\mathbb{Q}\)) extends to the real line \(\mathbb{R}\). The Jordan measurerable sets extends to the Lebesgue measurable sets ( \(\sigma -algebra\) ).

The outer measure can measure all subsets of \(X\), where as measure can only measure a \(\sigma -algebra\) of measure set. The Carathéodory measurability defines the condittion to make a \(\sigma -algebra\).
\[ \mu^*(A) = \mu^*(A \cap E) + \mu^*(A \cap E^c) \] The Carathéodory extension theorem defines a condition to make an outer measure to an measure. The condition is that the outer measure applys to the Carathéodory measurable set (\(\sigma - algebra\)). (Torence Tao, An introduction to measure theory)

In the Riesz representation therorem, \(\mathfrak{M}_F\) extends to \(\mathfrak{M}\). The outer measure is \(\mu(E) = sup\ \ \{\mu(K): K \subset E,\ \ K \ \ compact \}\) for __every \(E \subset X\) analogus to Jordan outer measure. \(\mathfrak{M}_F\) is collection of subset \(E \subset X\) satisfying \(\mu (E) < \infty\) and \(\mu (V) = sup \{\Lambda f:f\prec V \}\) analogus to Jordan inner measure. Thus \(\mathfrak{M}_F\) is analous to Jordan measurable set. \(\mathfrak{M}\) is collection of subset \(E \subset X\) such that \(E \cap K \ \in \ \ \mathfrak{M}_F\) for every compact \(K\). This is the Carathéodory measurability. So the \(\mu(E)\) on the \(\mathfrak{M}\) becomes measure. (Rudin’s Real and complex analysis)

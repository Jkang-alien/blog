This is a note for Elements of information theory of Thomas M. Cover.

The entropy (\(H\)) is a measure of uncertainty of a variable which is the answer of what is the ultimate data compression. Is the conditional probability \(p(x|y)\) considered as a probability of the “conditional variable” \((X|Y=y)\). Yes, It is the subset of \(X\) given \(Y=y\). If you sum all of the subset probabilities, it becomes the cardinality of \(X\). Thus If you make that become 1, the conditional probability should be multipied with \(p(x)\). The conditional probablity is larger than joint probabilities \(p(x,y)\).

Let’s think about the entropy of a joint random variable (\((X,Y)\)). If \(X\) and \(Y\) are correlated \(p(x,y)\), the entries of the contigent table of \(p(x,y)\) are concentrated at some points that means lager or smaller probablities than product of \(p(x)\) and \(p(y)\). The joint probability is a product of probability of \(X\) (\(p(x)\)) and conditional probability \(p(X|Y)\). The conditional entropy is expectation of random variable (conditional entropy) is . The conditional entropy does not means the entropy of subset of \(X|Y=y\). It is a measure of uncertainty of \(X\) given \(Y\). If \(Y\) has the information of the \(X\), the entropy of \(X|Y\) is less than \(X\). The conditional entropy \(H(X|Y)\) is substract the entropy of Y \(H(Y)\) from joint entropy \(H(X,Y)\). The joint probability \(p(x,y)\) is the product of probability of \(Y\) and conditional probability \(p(x|Y=y)\). This is chain rule of joint entropy \(H(X,Y) = H(Y) + H(X|Y)\).

The chain rule is converting joint variable to sum of conditional random vairiables. The joint variable is sum of conditional random variables. This is can be applied in the entropy, the information and the relative entropy.

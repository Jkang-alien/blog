\[ f(x) = \sum_{k=0}^\infty c_k x^k = c_0 + c_1 x + c_2 x^2 + \dotsb. \]

This is apporoximation which is function of h and derivatives of \(f(x)\) are elements of parameters.

$ f(x h) = f(x) hf’(x) + f’’(x) f^{(3)}(x) + O(h^4)$

Let’s think about \(\sin(x)\).

\[\begin{align*} f(x) &= \sin(x) & f(0) &= 0 f'(x) &= \cos(x) & f'(0) &= 1 f(x) &= -\sin(x) & f(0) &= 0 f(x) &= -\cos(x) & f(0) &= -1 & \vdots \end{align*}\]

Thus,

\[\begin{align*} \sin(x) &= 0 + \frac{1}{1!}x + \frac{0}{2!}x^2 + \frac{-1}{3!}x^3 + \dotsb &= x - \frac{x^3}{3!} + \frac{x^5}{5!} - \dotsb, \end{align*}\]

This is approximation. Now \(x\) becomes \(h\) and parameters calculated from derivatives of \(f(x)\) at \(0\).
\(f(x \pm h) = f(x) \pm hf'(x) + \frac{h^2}{2}f''(x) \pm \frac{h^3}{6}f^{(3)}(x) + O(h^4)\)

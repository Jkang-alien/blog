This is a note of An introduction to measure theory, Terence Tao https://terrytao.wordpress.com/books/an-introduction-to-measure-theory/. In the extended non-negative real line, an infinite series \(\Sigma_{n=1}^{\infty} x_{n} \in [0, \infty]\) is always convergent as a limit of the partial sum \(\Sigma_{n=1}^{N} x_{n}\).

The Jordan measure is an infimum of the finite sum of element measure. The Lebegue measure is an infimum of the infinite sum of element measure. the questions are whether the infimum a kind of limit, and what is difference between the Lebegue measure and as a limit of Jordan measure.
